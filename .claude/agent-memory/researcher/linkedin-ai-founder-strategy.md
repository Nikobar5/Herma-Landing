# LinkedIn Content Strategy for AI Founders (2026)

Researched: 2026-03-24

## Content Formats (ranked by engagement)

1. **Document/Carousel posts** — 6.6% avg engagement, 4x better than text, 278% better than video. Native PDF/carousel format maximizes dwell time (every swipe = retention signal).
2. **Native video (30-90s)** — 5.6% avg engagement, growing 36% YoY. Short video > long.
3. **Text-only** — 2-4% engagement, but still viable if hook is exceptional. Requires strong first 150 chars.
4. Single images underperform; avoid as primary format.

## Hook Styles That Work

- **Contrarian:** "Everyone thinks X. Here's why they're wrong."
- **Data lead:** "[Specific number] we spent on LLM costs last month. Here's what we learned."
- **Mid-story:** Start in the middle of a narrative, not the beginning.
- **Vulnerability + result:** "I almost shut down Herma last month. Then this happened."
- Rule: first 210 characters = only what matters before "see more" cutoff. Hook's only job = earn next 5 seconds.

## Optimal Post Length

- Sweet spot: 150-300 words for text posts
- Carousels: 5-10 slides, each slide one clear point
- NO external links in body (60% reach reduction). Put links in comments after posting.

## Content Mix

- 40% educational (how-to, frameworks, technical explainers)
- 30% personal/founder story
- 20% industry insights / hot takes
- 10% behind-the-scenes / product updates

## Engagement Patterns

- Comments carry 15x more algorithmic weight than likes — design posts to spark comments, not just reactions.
- Educational content gets 3-5x more reach than other types.
- Personal story posts get 5x more comments.
- First 60 minutes after posting are critical — respond to every comment in the first hour (+35% visibility boost).
- External links → put in first comment, not the post body.

## Posting Cadence

- Optimal: 3-5 posts per week. Never 2 posts in 24 hours on same profile (they cannibalize each other).
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday
- Best times: 7-9 AM and 10-11 AM (target audience timezone)
- "Post every day" is now penalized in 2026 — quality > volume.

## Hashtag Strategy

- Use 3-5 hashtags per post (sweet spot). More = diminishing returns.
- Hashtag following was removed in late 2024 — hashtags now serve as topic signals for LinkedIn's LLM classifier, not distribution channels.
- Mix: 1-2 broad tags + 2-3 niche tags.
- Recommended for Herma/Niko:
  - Broad: #ArtificialIntelligence, #MachineLearning, #TechStartups
  - Niche: #LLM, #AIInfrastructure, #DeveloperTools, #AIStartups, #LargeLanguageModels
  - Avoid: #Technology, #Innovation (too generic, high competition, low targeting value)

## What NOT to Do

- Engagement pods → actively penalized by LinkedIn's 2026 AI classifiers, can cause shadowban
- Generic polls → penalized
- Daily posting → penalized
- External links in post body → 60% reach penalty
- Mass cold DMs → 79% of B2B decision-makers ignore them

## Algorithm (2026 Update)

LinkedIn rebuilt its feed with LLMs + transformer-based "Universal Alpha" model. It now prioritizes:
- Dwell time > vanity metrics
- Topic/niche authority (consistent topic = better distribution)
- Conversation depth (contextual comments > generic "great post")
- Rapid first-hour engagement signals

## Niko-Specific Content Angles

1. **Data post:** "We routed 10,000 LLM requests last week. Here's where 65% of spend was wasted." → carousel
2. **Technical explainer:** "EV-based routing for LLMs — why expected value beats fixed model selection" → carousel
3. **Founder story:** "I'm a GT junior building an AI company from my dorm. Here's what 3 months of no revenue taught me."
4. **Contrarian:** "AI cost optimization advice is mostly wrong. Here's what actually cuts bills."
5. **Open-source angle:** "We open-sourced our LLM eval toolkit. Here's what we learned building it."
6. **Hot take:** "Not Diamond / RouteLLM solve the wrong problem. Routing isn't about the model — it's about confidence."
